Pad Thai

Pad Thai is a stir-fried rice noodle dish commonly served as a street food and at casual local eateries in Thailand. It is made with soaked dried rice noodles, which are stir-fried with eggs and chopped firm tofu, and flavored with tamarind pulp, fish sauce, dried shrimp, garlic or shallots, red chili pepper and palm sugar, and served with lime wedges and often chopped roasted peanuts.

Ingredients

  • 1 (8 ounce) package rice noodles
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 12 ounces extra-firm tofu, cut into 1/2 inch cubes
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/4 cup ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/8 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/8 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground tur

Steps

  1. Soak rice noodles in a large bowl of hot water until tender, about 30 minutes. Drain.
  2. Heat oil in a large skillet or wok over medium heat. Add tofu; cook until browned, 6 to 8 minutes. Remove from skillet and drain on paper towels.
  3. Combine soy sauce, water, ketchup, sugar, paprika, pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, and turmeric in a bowl.
  4. Heat the same skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add noodles and soy sauce mixture; cook and stir until noodles are coated, 3 to 5 minutes.
  5. Stir tofu into the noodles. Cook until heated through, about 5 minutes.
